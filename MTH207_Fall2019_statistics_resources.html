In May, 1996 the world changed when Tim Berners-Lee submitted RFC 1945 in which is the foundation of the Worldwide Web.

Over time, the web has evolved into a very rich source of information on almost any subject.

This web page lists some good resources for introductory statistics.

Onlinestatbook Interactive Multimedia Statistics Course

This is a free, public domain online resource for teaching and learning introductory statistics.

  • You can find the Onlinestatbook project home page here.
  • Onlinestatbook was developed at Rice University, the University of Houston Clear Lake, and Tufts University.
  • On the project home page, you will find links to the the following resources:
    • The interactive textbook
    • An interactive Apple e-book (for IOS and OSx)
    • A PDF version of the textbook
    • An e-Pub electronic book version
    • A link to the Rice Virtual Lab in Statistics including simulations and case studies

The proper citation for Onlinestatsbook is:

Online Statistics Education: A Multimedia Course of Study (http://onlinestatbook.com/). Project Leader: David M. Lane, Rice University.

Khan Academy Statistics and Probability

If you prefer videos, Khan Academy Statistics and Probability collection provides well-made, easy to follow videos that walk you through concepts and examples.
